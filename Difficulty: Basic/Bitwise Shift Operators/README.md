<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/bitwise-right-shift/1?page=1&category=CPP&difficulty=Basic,Easy&status=unsolved&sortBy=submissions">Bitwise Shift Operators</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Basic</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO" style="--text-color: var(--problem-text-color);"><p><span style="font-size: 18px;">Given two positive integers <strong>a</strong> and <strong>b</strong>, perform a <strong data-path-to-node="3" data-index-in-node="47">right bitwise shift</strong> of a by b positions, followed by a <strong data-path-to-node="3" data-index-in-node="102">left bitwise shift</strong> of a by b positions. Print both results separated by a space, </span><span style="font-size: 18px;">followed by</span><span style="font-size: 18px;"> new line</span><span style="font-size: 18px;">.</span></p>
<p><strong style="font-size: 18px;">Examples:</strong></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input</strong>: a = 5, b = 2
<strong>Output:</strong> 1 20
<strong>Explanation</strong>: 5 in binary is 101. Shifting right by 2 gives 001, which equals 1. Shifting left by 2 gives 10100, which equals 20.</span> </pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>a = 4, b = 1
<strong>Output:</strong> 2 8
<strong>Explanation</strong>:<span style="font-size: 14pt;"> 4</span></span><span style="font-size: 14pt;"> in binary is 100. Shifting right by 1 gives 010, which equals 2. Shifting left by 1 gives 1000, which equals 8.</span> </pre>
